Italy — Heat, Cities & Pilgrim Houses

Late June 2025 · Northern Italy · Turin · Cuneo

Italian sun with no mercy. A pilgrim house as shelter. Turin for a day. Then Cuneo — and the Alps waiting again on the horizon.

Italy hit differently. After the cool alpine air of Switzerland, the Po Valley was a wall of heat — radiating off the tarmac, shimmering in the distance, relentless and magnificent. The landscape flattened out, the road stretched ahead in long straight lines, and the sun turned everything golden. Cycling in Italy is beautiful and brutal in equal measure.

Accommodation along this stretch came in the form of a pilgrim house — the kind of simple, no-questions-asked shelter that exists for walkers and cyclists doing long routes. A bed, four walls, a shared bathroom. Everything you actually need when you've been riding all day under 35°C. I slept immediately.

Turin was a detour worth every kilometre. A full day off the bike — proper streets, real coffee, a museum visit, long evenings at outdoor tables. The city felt enormous after days of mountains and small villages. Then came Cuneo: a quieter Italian city at the foot of the Maritime Alps, where the route turned south-west and the mountains appeared again on the horizon. The second crossing was coming. But first — rest.
