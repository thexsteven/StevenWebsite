Second Alpine Crossing — 2,700 m

Late June / Early July 2025 · Maritime Alps

No proper path. Loose rock. Carrying the bike on my back for hours. And then a night at 2,700 metres — alone, exhausted, and somehow completely at peace.

The second Alpine crossing was in a different category entirely. The first crossing — Splügen — had been a proper mountain pass with a real road. This one, in the Maritime Alps between Italy and France, was something else. The mapped route disappeared into loose rock and steep scree. No tarmac. No path. Just the mountain.

I ended up carrying the bike. Lifting it over rocks, pushing it up slopes too steep to ride, occasionally questioning every decision that had led to this moment. The weight of the bike plus loaded panniers on loose terrain at altitude is its own kind of suffering. The kind that's very difficult to explain but very easy to remember.

What nobody tells you about suffering through something like that is how quiet the top of a mountain is. At 2,700 metres, in an emergency shelter with no phone signal and no other people, the silence is absolute. The night was cold, the sleeping bag earned its rating, and I lay there looking at a sky full of stars through a gap in the shelter door. France was on the other side. One more descent to go.

  • Altitude 2,700 m overnight
  • Terrain Loose rock · No path
  • Method Carried the bike by hand
  • Overnight Emergency mountain shelter
