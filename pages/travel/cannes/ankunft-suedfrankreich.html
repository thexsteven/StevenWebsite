Cycling Tour Konstanz → Cannes

Arrival — Southern France & Cannes

Early July 2025 · Nice → Cannes

The descent into Nice. The first sight of the Mediterranean. And then Cannes — the finish line that didn't feel like an ending, but like a beginning.

After the second Alpine crossing, the descent into France was one of the most euphoric experiences of my life. Every metre of altitude lost was kilometres of suffering paid back. The air got warmer. The vegetation changed — pine trees giving way to Mediterranean scrub, then olive trees, then palms. And then, through a gap in the hills, the Mediterranean appeared. Blue, enormous, utterly indifferent to the fact that I had cycled almost 900 km to get here.

Nice was overwhelming in the best way. After days of mountains, small villages, emergency shelters and railway bridges, the Promenade des Anglais hit like a different planet. People in linen shirts. Restaurants with actual tables. The sea just... there. I sat on the beach for a long time with the bike lying in the sand beside me and did absolutely nothing.

The final stretch from Nice to Cannes runs along the coast — the Côte d'Azur at its most famous. By the time I rolled into Cannes, the legs had stopped communicating entirely, and I didn't care. A friend was waiting. The journey was done. 900 kilometres, 4 countries, 2 Alpine crossings, 1 railway bridge, and 1 pilgrim house. Not bad for someone with no prior cycling experience.

  • Final City Cannes, France 🇫🇷
  • Via Nice · Côte d'Azur
  • Total ~900 km · ~10 days
